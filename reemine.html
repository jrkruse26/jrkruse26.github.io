GE Rare Earth Element Mine

Project Overview

Project goal: To develop a Rare Earth Element Mine to extract ore from a depth of 3000 meters while using alternative, environmentally friendly energy sources

Team Size: 3 students

Team mission statement: To work as a team in order to design and develop products that adhere to environmental concerns and financial restraints.

Project Specs and Constraints

  • The Rare-Earth Element Concentration is uniform throughout the ore deposit
  • The Rare-Earth Ore is worth $10,000 per ton extracted
  • Assume that each draw point provides a continuous flow of ore and that there are no hang-ups, downtime, or large boulders to break up during the extraction process.
  • There are 100 Draw Points in the Mine
  • Ore Deposit is a cylinder 1,000 meters tall and 2,200 meters in diameter
  • The deposit is 9.5% REE ore

Timeline:

  • Project Assigned: March 13th, 2017
  • Final Presentation: April 24th, 2017

Defining Specifications

We developed a table of customer specifications based of rules, regulation, and performance requirements that we would work with in order to best meet the needs of the client devloped

Catagory Specificaion
Airborne Contamination
  • The percentage of airborne contaminations in the mine’s air cannot exceed levels of:
    • Diesel fumes - 160 μg/m3 carbon
    • Carbon Monoxide (CO) - 2.5%
    • Hydrogen (H) - 0.8%
    • Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S) - .8%
    • Acetylene (C2H2) - 0.4%
    • Propane (C3H8) - 0.4%
    • Sulfur Dioxide (S02) - 5.0 ppm
    • Methane (CH4) - 1.0%
Stewardship
  • 0% of REE is sucessfully extracted from ore y
Environmental management
  • Appropriate measures will be undertaken to minimize mine run-offs into nearby rivers
  • Tailings are properly processed in order to avoid environmental contamination
Occupational health and workplace safety
  • Workers are familiar with underground ore mining process
  • No more than two injuries per month
  • Toxic gas particulates will never reach levels that could harm human life
  • Mine must have two or more separate, properly maintained escapeways
Operating costs and productivity
  • Mine Generates profit of at least $300,000 per week
Local community engagement
  • Keep community informed through local news
  • No disturbing surrounding communities
  • Collaborate with communities to address concerns
Waste management and disposal
  • Excess rocks (including basalt) can be processed to be sold as construction materials
Mine closure and rehabilitation
  • Ensure proper signage around abandoned mine
  • Waste storage structure should remain stable for a minimum of 100-200 years
  • Proper measures will be taken to seal off mine, including fences around mine and locked doors on all entrances.
